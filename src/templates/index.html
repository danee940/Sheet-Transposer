How the chord transposer works

Transposing a song means shifting every chord by the same interval so it can be played or sung in a more comfortable key without changing the melody. This tool reads the chords in your Word (.docx) chord sheet, moves each one from your current key to the key you choose, and gives you back a file that is identical except for the new chords.

  1. 1 Upload your chord sheet in .docx format.
  2. 2 Select the key the song is currently written in.
  3. 3 Select the key you want to play it in.
  4. 4 Download the transposed sheet as a .docx or PDF.

Supported keys and chord notation

Every major and minor key is supported, including both sharp and flat spellings such as C#, Db, F#m, and Bbm. Chord qualities like minor, seventh, sus, add, maj7, and slash chords (for example G/B) are recognised and moved correctly. German H notation, where H stands for B and B stands for B-flat, is also detected and transposed accurately, so sheets written in either the English or German system work out of the box.

Frequently asked questions

Does transposing keep my document formatting? +

Yes. Only the chord names are changed. Fonts, spacing, colours, tables, and the overall layout of your .docx are preserved.

Is the chord transposer free? +

Yes, it is completely free with no sign-up. You can transpose as many chord sheets as you like.

Can I download the result as a PDF? +

Yes. Choose the PDF output option before transposing to get a ready-to-print PDF, or keep the editable .docx version.

Are my files stored anywhere? +

No. Your chord sheet is processed to generate the transposed file and is not kept after the download is returned.
